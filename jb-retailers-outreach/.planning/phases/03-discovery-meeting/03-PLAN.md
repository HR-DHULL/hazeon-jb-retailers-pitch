# PHASE 03 PLAN: Discovery Meeting — Listen First, Pitch Later

## Objective
Run a 30–45 minute discovery meeting with Ali Khan. Goal: Understand his world, surface his pain, identify his ambition. You should talk 20% of the time.

## Meeting Format
**Preferred:** In-person at his Sector 15D store (home turf = he's comfortable)
**Fallback:** WhatsApp video call (5 minutes to connect, then screen share)
**Duration:** 30–45 minutes max (respect his time — he's running 3 stores)

---

## Opening (First 2 Minutes)
```
"Ali bhai, thank you for the time. Main sirf kuch cheezein dikhana chahta tha
jo humne already kar diya hai aapke liye — aur phir kuch sawaal poochhunga.
Koi pressure nahi, no pitch. Just a conversation."

[Show the 5 quick wins on your laptop — 5 minutes max]
→ TripAdvisor claimed
→ Google Maps fixed (3 stores)
→ Link-in-bio live
→ 30-day content calendar
→ Competitor audit
```

**Watch his reaction.** If he leans forward, he's interested. If he nods and says "haan yaar, yeh toh hona chahiye tha" — he's sold on the idea, just needs the details.

---

## Discovery Questions (Ask These — In Order)

### Understanding His Business
1. "JB Retailers ko 13 saal ho gaye — aapka growth kaise raha? Teeno stores hai abhi, aur plan kya hai aage ke liye?"
   *(Listen for: expansion plans, revenue growth, any plateaus)*

2. "Abhi customers mainly kahan se aate hain? Sector 15 wale? Mohali se? Social media se koi aata hai?"
   *(Listen for: how dependent he is on foot traffic)*

3. "Instagram pe 32K followers hain — kya unse koi direct business aata hai? Koi order liya kisi ne DM pe?"
   *(Listen for: whether he sees Instagram as a sales channel or just brand awareness)*

### Surfacing the Pain
4. "Kya kabhi hua hai ki koi customer Instagram pe product dekhe aur phir purchase na kar sake — kyunki koi link ya website nahi thi?"
   *(This is the ₹1 Crore question. He will say yes.)*

5. "Festival season mein — Eid, Diwali, New Year — kitna rush hota hai? Kya store pe handle ho pata hai ya customers chale jaate hain?"
   *(Listen for: demand exceeding physical capacity = online solves this)*

6. "Koi competitor hai jo online bhi kar raha ho same products? Unhe koi problem hoti hai?"
   *(Listen for: competitor awareness + pain of losing customers online)*

### Budget & Readiness
7. "Abhi digital pe kuch spend ho raha hai? Instagram boost, Google ads, kuch?"
   *(Establishes current spend baseline — if ₹5K/month, our ₹35K looks big; if ₹20K, it looks smart)*

8. "Agar hum aapka pura digital setup kar dein — website, WhatsApp orders, Instagram automation — aapke team mein koi hai jo isko manage kar sake ya sab humse chahiye?"
   *(Identifies if they need full-service or just tools)*

9. "Agar 6 mahine mein ₹10 Lakh ka online business ho jaye JB Retailers ka — toh aap uspe kitna invest karne ke liye comfortable hain?"
   *(Budget anchoring without naming a price first)*

### The Closer Question
10. "Agar aaj main ek button dabaunga aur kal se JB Retailers online ho jaye — aapko sabse pehle kya chahiye? Website? WhatsApp orders? Instagram pe shopping?"
    *(His answer = exactly what to lead with in the proposal)*

Show the comparison table. Let the silence do the work after showing:

---

## Reading the Room — What to Watch For

| Signal | Meaning | Your Response |
|---|---|---|
| "Haan yaar, yeh toh karna chahiye tha" | High intent, self-convinced | Move to proposal immediately |
| "Kitna lagega?" | Buying signal — he's thinking about it | "Depend karta hai scope pe — cover karte hain next step mein" |
| "Pehle ek do cheezein try karte hain" | Risk-averse — suggest Starter Package | "Bilkul — chhota start karte hain, results dekh ke badhao" |
| "Mere paas time nahi" | Objection — fear of effort | "Isiliye automation hai — aapko kuch nahi karna" |
| "Pehle soch lene do" | Need more proof/trust | "Koi baat nahi — ek week aur free kaam karte hain" |
| "Budget tight hai" | Price objection | "ROI dikha deta hoon — pehle number dekho" |
